## Historical Context

**The USS Nimitz Incident (November 14, 2004)**

On November 14, 2004, CDR David Fravor (VFA-41) and other Navy pilots operating from the USS Nimitz conducted an encounter with an unidentified craft. Key facts:

- **Location:** Pacific Ocean off Southern California coast
- **Date:** November 14, 2004
- **Participants:** F/A-18 pilots from USS Nimitz Carrier Strike Group
- **Duration:** Approximately 5 minutes of visual contact
- **Object Description:** White, elongated (approx. 47 feet), wingless, "Tic-Tac" shaped
- **Radar Data:** Tracked 60 miles away after visual contact ended
- **Declassification:** Official Pentagon release June 2020
- **Investigator:** CDR David Fravor (16 years flight experience, retired since)

## Notes on Content

### FLIR1 Video
The FLIR1 footage is authentic official government video from the Navy's Forward-Looking Infrared system mounted on an F/A-18. This was officially declassified and released by the Pentagon as part of the 2020 UFO video release initiative. The footage shows the thermal signature of the unidentified object during the encounter.

### Executive Report
The PDF contains official briefing material and analysis of the incident. While marked as an executive report, it represents compiled government documentation of the encounter.

### Illustrations vs. Official Footage
Note that the Tic-Tac illustration (01-USS_Nimitz_2004_tic_tac_UFO_wikimedia.jpg) is an artistic representation created in 2018 based on pilot descriptions. The FLIR footage (03) is actual recorded thermal imagery from the encounter.
