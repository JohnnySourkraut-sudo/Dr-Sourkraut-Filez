## The USS Nimitz Incident (November 14, 2004)

### What Happened

On November 14, 2004, pilots from the USS Nimitz Carrier Strike Group operating off the coast of Southern California encountered an unidentified aerial phenomenon.

**Key Participants:**
- **CDR David Fravor** (VFA-41 Commander) - Primary witness
- **Other Navy pilots** - Additional visual witnesses
- **USS Princeton** - Radar tracking vessel
- **USS Nimitz** - Carrier with aircraft

**Incident Duration:** ~5 minutes of visual contact

### Object Description

- **Shape:** Elongated/oval (resembled a "Tic-Tac" candy)
- **Size:** Approximately 47 feet (14 meters) long
- **Color:** White
- **Features:** Wingless, no visible propulsion system
- **Associated Phenomena:** Sea surface disturbance beneath the object
- **Movement:** Erratic, rapidly accelerating

### Radar Data

- Tracked by USS Princeton's sophisticated radar system
- Initial altitude: 15,000-25,000 feet
- Disappeared from visual contact
- Reappeared on radar 60 miles away at the pilot's Combat Air Patrol (CAP) point
- Movements defied conventional aircraft physics

### Historical Significance

- **2004:** Original incident (classified)
- **June 2020:** Pentagon officially declassified FLIR video
- **2021:** Congressional briefings on UAP incidents
- **Ongoing:** Subject of continued official government investigation
